Only when our lived experiences begin to overlap do seeing and understanding really begin. We can look at people through a city, or come to understand a city—and the cultures braided beneath it—through people. Compared with a city, a person is the smallest window onto the world.

In a co-living community, it might be idle tea in a kitchen at dawn; it might be the helplessness of facing a mess after an event. Different souls knock against each other in these small details. They became anchors, letting me rebuild a feeling of home with the social world.

Even while drifting everywhere, you know there are things that hold you: people you love, landscapes you cannot stand, maybe the familiar argument, maybe the laughter that follows.
