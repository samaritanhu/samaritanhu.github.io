A friend of more than ten years texted me once: “Fuma is a little like Akana Rika in *Tokyo Love Story*. Cute.”

This was before I had backpacked through Japan. I was alone in the Victorian living room of the mushroom house in San Francisco, drinking Hibiki and finishing *Tokyo Love Story*.

I was stunned by her appetite for life, her refusal of the expected, her habit of leaving home every day with a whole backpack of curiosity. Rika, too, is sensitive, a little avoidant, a girl who keeps moving because stillness asks too much.

Maybe that is why she never felt fictional to me. She felt alive beside me—alive inside my body.
