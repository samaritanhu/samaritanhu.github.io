Three whole years in San Francisco.

At first I joined City Walks: the coastal forts; the Presidio and the history of Spanish invasion; the wartime incarceration camps and the long, uneven lives of Japanese Americans on the West Coast; Japantown's Cherry Blossom Parade; Chinatown, the railroad built by Chinese laborers, and the legal history Chinese Americans helped force into being—the idea that to be born here is to belong here.

Then, one step at a time, I began measuring the city with my feet and became a small part of it. I started hosting: cable cars that keep beating at the city's center twenty-four hours a day; the old streetcars broken by 1906 and Muni reborn in 1980; sourdough tastings in Alamo Square Park.

Maybe I had only survived in the city before. Slowly, by breathing its air each day, I began to grow *L. sanfranciscensis* somewhere inside me. My body accepted this strange, feral fermentation and joined the place. Pink sunsets, San Francisco's sudden crying weather, the minor miracles of a parade or a strange event: all of it interrupts the plan, and makes it fresher.

Fuma has been growing up with this city, too.
