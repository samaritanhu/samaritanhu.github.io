# Design document — A Salon for Deep Questions

## Intent

This is not a résumé page or a scrolling social feed. It is a quiet personal archive: part research notebook, part travel ledger, part room for conversation. The visual language takes inspiration from the calm editorial pace and museum-like restraint of lijin.org, while remaining specific to Fuma's life.

## Narrative order

1. **Personal fieldwork** — a mountain lake holds the opening statement: technical work and a searching life share the same frame.
2. **Three rooms** — thinking, field notes, and the salon give visitors a legible way into the site.
3. **Question garden** — research is presented as a live question rather than a list of credentials.
4. **Field notes** — exactly two paired records: solitude in landscape and belonging in community.
5. **The salon** — a dark reading room introduces literature, philosophy, and deep connection.
6. **Film wall** — a final, grainy afterimage rather than a conventional footer gallery.

## Visual system

| Element | Choice |
| --- | --- |
| Palette | Warm paper, near-black ink, moss, and a restrained oxide accent. |
| Type | High-contrast serif for reflective text; compact monospaced labels for archive metadata. |
| Space | Large blank intervals, thin rules, and asymmetric editoral grids. |
| Photography | First-party film/digital images; soft desaturation, light sepia, and subtle grain. |
| Motion | Only brief hover shifts. No auto-playing media or decorative animation. |

## Responsive behavior

Desktop uses a two-column field-note layout and long horizontal film wall. At 720px and below, navigation remains compact, the hero stays tall enough for the statement, field notes stack into a readable single column, and the film wall becomes one image. No hover interaction is required to understand the page.

## Content rules

- A Field Note must have one image, one location, one short observation, and a source/date.
- Add a third Field Note only when it opens a distinct theme; do not add photos merely to fill the grid.
- Keep professional work framed through questions and essays rather than job-search language.
- The source link credits the original social post without depending on it to render the photograph.
